The GA4 Detection Gap

"If traffic appears in GA but not in FouAnalytics, that traffic didn't actually occur."

Most analytics platforms are required by internet standards to filter bot traffic — which means you never see it. Bots are removed from view before you can act on them. FouAnalytics runs as a supplementary layer, revealing every bot category your existing tools hide: bad bots (red), declared bots (orange), and search crawlers (yellow).

How It Works

Deterministic, not probabilistic.

Every visit is classified server-side using 300+ parameters. The classification happens where bots can't observe it — there is no feedback loop for an attacker to A/B test against.

Step 01

Install the Tag

Two lines of code or Google Tag Manager. No cookies. No PII collected or needed. GDPR conformant by design.

Step 02

See the Bots

Every visit is classified using 300+ server-side parameters. If three or more red flags are present, the visit is marked dark red — a bot. Red flags include:

datacenter IP no GPU detected no plugins screen res mismatch 1×1 window click at (0,0) tab invisible no mouse move

About FouAnalytics

FouAnalytics was built to solve a problem the industry wouldn't. In 2012, founder Dr. Augustine Fou — then at Omnicom — discovered click-through rates greater than 100% in pharmaceutical client campaigns. Fraud hidden by averages, driven by fake sites designed to mimic legitimate publishers. Advertisers assumed their campaigns were performing well. The platforms, adtech vendors, and agencies let them believe it, because it meant they'd keep increasing spend. Existing analytics tools couldn't explain what he was seeing — so he built his own tag.

The platform installs in two lines of code. No cookies. No PII. GDPR conformant. Every visit is classified using 300+ browser and server-side parameters: blue (human), red (bad bot), orange (declared bot), yellow (search crawler). Classification is deterministic — not probabilistic. It happens server-side, so it cannot be observed by an attacker. There is no feedback loop for bots to A/B test against. UTM parameters are auto-parsed so bot traffic traces directly back to its paid media source.

FouAnalytics has been self-funded since 2012. No VCs. No investors. No board. No quarterly earnings to manage. No financial pressure to soften findings, protect relationships, or hide inconvenient numbers. The platform exists to show what other tools obscure — not to protect the interests of the parties causing the fraud.

Dr. Fou holds a PhD from MIT and has published 1000+ articles and slide decks on ad fraud and digital media accountability. FouAnalytics works with advertisers and publishers who actually want to know the truth — and who have the courage to do something when they find it.
